Introduction & Approach

02 / Gucci past & present

Guccification

Under direction of Alessandro Michele, the retail experience has returned to a pre-Tom Ford moment. Maximalist, low-fi 20th century cues convey the warmth of the original leatherworks.

Key takeaways

1. Surprise & Discovery

Shop Attendants steward brand heritage and keep the “Cult of the Artist” alive with information about past creative directors of the house. Tom Ford product "Touchability"

  • Products Staged to be Handled
  • Chance Discovery & Unrepeatability
  • Collateral Materials Readily Available

2. Time travel & archive

As with the collection, the materials used across the Milan flagship store are borrowed from a dizzying array of eras, creating a sense of disorientation

  • Textured cream walls, brass fixtures
  • Stark lighting cues downmarket
  • Early 60’s boutique / 80’s mall

3. Theater & Transformation

Explicit theater cues include stage lights and dressing screens. The selling ceremony is a co-creative free play of magical symbols and precious materials

  • Dionysian Hedonism, Costume
  • Occult Magic, Shamanism, Fetish
  • Role-play, Parody, Cross Dressing

4.Made in italy

While some of the precious skins are sourced from around the world, Leathergoods, Printed Silk and Wool,Metallic Jersey are all printed and produced in Italy

  • Verona - Printing & Knitwear
  • Como - Silk Production & Printing
  • Tuscany- Leatherwork
