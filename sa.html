The Future of Premium Travel

I was commissioned to conduct cultural analysis and trends research in luxury and premium travel across six major markets to identify creative routes and design execution guidelines for First and Business Class in-Cabin experience design. I led an international team of analysts in Paris, Milan, Los Angeles, New York, Shanghai, Doha and Singapore, synthesized findings across markets to develop creative guidelines for interior materials, lighting, color palette, staff uniform, amenities kit and in-cabin content for the London based design team.

Implications for Premium Travel


  • 01.
    Ultra Luxe

    Exclusive access to one of a kind experiences are part of inner-focused vision of ‘ultra luxury’ that can be credibly leveraged

  • 02.
    Indigenous Luxury

    Protectionism in global trade is reflected by rising support of local economies and increased desire for materials sourced for their unique provenance

  • 03.
    Eastern Promise

    Rise of regional creative industries and designers in Southeast Asia and Africa can inspire in-cabin details and collaborations for wardrobe and amenity kits

  • 04.
    Global Pick & Mix

    Increased interconnectivity is leading to disruptive juxtapositions between Eastern & Western styles, craftsmanship and materials

  • 05.
    Legacy Service

    Legacy service, traditional materials associated with more formal times include shoe shining (St. Regis), wet shaves (Dunhill), tailoring or laundry services

  • 06.
    Augmented Nature

    Responsive materials and in-cabin environment designed for wellbeing simulate natural ambience for continuous, preventative wellness.

03 / Strategic insight

Luxury narratives across all markets were shifting toward stories about cultural preservation. In markets such as Singapore and China, regional fashion brands were increasingly displayed as symbols of national pride and soft power. After identifying the richest opportunity areas relevant for all markets, we developed creative routes to inform every detail of First and Business class experience.

1.
DEEP LUXURY

All markets we studied displayed a macro shift away from Exhibitionism and Ultraluxury Megabrands as status symbols in favour of more discreet, deeply personal and unrepeatable experiences. Within this more subtle and holistic view of luxury, limited resources and slow production process have become the core message of emergent luxury narrative.

Philippe Rahm

2.
AUGMENTED NATURE

Advances in smart materials innovation and growing mainstream concern about environmental impact has led to an embrace of AI to better understand and respond to the impact of noise and materials on hormones & energy levels. The rise of wearable, responsive technology and materials is shifting paradigms of wellness from focus on personal indulgence and health managed by self will toward scientifically grounded approaches to designed environments which are informed by progressive learning and AI.

3.
GLOBAL PICK & MIX

Previously marginalized regions are coming into the forefront as luxury retail development expands into new areas of growth. Tokyo, Shanghai, Hong Kong and Seoul have been luxury capitals in the region and now Singapore, Kuala Lampur and others are proving to be robust luxury markets.
